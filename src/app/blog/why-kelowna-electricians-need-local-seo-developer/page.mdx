## What most Kelowna electrician websites get wrong

1. **One page called "services" that lists everything.** Panel upgrade, EV charger, solar, knob and tube replacement, service calls, commercial. Each of these is a unique search and a unique customer. Bundled together, they rank for none.
2. **No mention of local neighborhoods or build styles.** The electrical needs of a 1960s Rutland rancher are not the same as a new build in the Ponds. Your site should reflect that.
3. **Slow loading photos of vans.** I do not need a 4MB hero image of your truck. I need it to load fast.
4. **No Google Business Profile integration.** Your GBP drives most of your local traffic and your site should reinforce it, with matching NAP, embedded reviews, and real project locations.
5. **No trust signals.** Licensed, bonded, insured, FSR. Put it in the header. Electrical is a trust business.

## What actually works for electricians in Kelowna

- A dedicated page for every high-value service: panel upgrades, EV charger installs, hot tub wiring, knob and tube, service upgrades. Each page targets a specific search.
- Neighborhood-level content. A post about EV charger installs in Lake Country. A guide to panel upgrades in Glenmore. Google rewards specificity.
- Fast-loading galleries of real jobs. Real panels, real labels, real Kelowna houses.
- Clear licensing and insurance info in the header and footer.
- Internal linking between service pages so someone who lands on "EV charger install" can find "panel upgrade" when they realize they need both.
