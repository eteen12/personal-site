Open any Kelowna realtor's website. Then open ten more. They all look the same. Same IDX feed embedded in an iframe, same "meet the team" page, same drone video of the lake. Same headshot on a lakeshore bench. If a buyer is comparing agents online, there is no reason for them to pick you over the next realtor on the list.

Real estate in Kelowna is a relationship business, sure. But the first impression happens online. And the first impression is almost always a template your brokerage picked for 200 agents.

## What most Kelowna realtor websites get wrong

1. **The brokerage template.** You and 199 other agents all using the same CMS. You cannot differentiate because you are not allowed to.
2. **IDX embeds that look terrible.** Slow, ugly, not responsive, not branded. A buyer lands on your site, clicks a listing, and gets dropped into a 2012-era MLS interface.
3. **No neighborhood pages.** Kelowna is not one market. Upper Mission is a different buyer than Rutland. Lake Country is different than downtown. A generic "Kelowna homes" page ranks for nothing.
4. **No real content.** Market updates from 18 months ago, or a blog that stopped in 2022. Google sees an abandoned site.
5. **Zero personality.** Buyers hire people, not logos. Your site should sound like you, show your actual face, and tell a real story.

## What actually works for realtors in Kelowna

- Neighborhood microsites or deep landing pages for the areas you actually farm. Upper Mission, Lower Mission, Glenmore, Lake Country, Kettle Valley, Wilden, Black Mountain, West Kelowna.
- Custom tools buyers actually use. Mortgage calculators that work on mobile. Comparison pages. Saved-search features. A valuation tool.
- Video content that is not a drone shot of the bridge. Walkthrough content, market commentary, neighborhood explainers.
- Fast, custom-designed pages. No brokerage template. Something that looks like a brand, not a directory entry.
- A content cadence. Monthly market updates with actual data, not canned copy.
