## 2. "Inner Harbour Web Agency" (local agency archetype)

Victoria's full-service shop. Nice office downtown, solid design sensibility, team of five to fifteen.

**Pros:** Design-forward, process-driven, fine for bigger stakeholder projects.

**Cons:** Expensive, slow, and often WordPress underneath the pretty front-end. If you need custom software, they'll subcontract it.

## 3. "Fernwood Freelance" (solo freelancer archetype)

Senior solo developer, probably in Fernwood or James Bay, charges real rates, books months out.

**Pros:** Excellent quality, direct contact, genuine senior-level work.

**Cons:** Expensive, booked solid, single-point-of-failure if they take a long weekend.

## 4. "Island Template Studio" (WordPress/Squarespace mill)

Flat-fee theme reseller. Two-week turnaround, Divi under the hood.

**Pros:** Cheap and fast.

**Cons:** In Victoria the audience will notice it's a template. Bounce rates will tell on you.

## 5. "Global Dev Outsource" (offshore archetype)

The offshore firm in your LinkedIn inbox.

**Pros:** Cheapest.

**Cons:** No Island context, weird copy, painful iteration across time zones.

## How to pick in Victoria

A quick decision tree:

- **Professional services firm (law, finance, wealth) wanting a brand refresh and a site.** The local agency is a fine call.
- **Growing service business that wants to rank, convert, and look sharp.** A senior freelancer or me. Skip the agency markup.
- **Tourism, hospitality, seasonal businesses.** You need speed-first engineering. Template shops can't deliver it.
- **Anything software — booking systems, member portals, custom apps, AI.** You want an engineer.

Most Victoria businesses I talk to fit bucket 2 or 4.
