## The "we can do that too" pitch

A marketing agency pitches a BC company on SEO, Google Ads, and social. Then someone asks, "can you do the website too?" The answer is always yes. Whether they actually have a developer on staff is a different question.

What usually arrives is a WordPress theme with the company logo dropped in, sold at custom-site prices.

## How marketing agencies do web

Their core competency is campaigns, content, and media buying. Those are real skills. Web is often a secondary offering handled by a junior person, a contractor, or a template the agency reuses across clients.

To be fair, some marketing agencies have excellent dev teams. Most do not. The red flag is when the same agency offers SEO, paid ads, social, branding, video, and web — with one small team.

## Where it breaks down

1. **No real engineering depth.** Custom features, integrations, and performance tuning require a developer who lives in code every day. Marketing generalists cannot do that.
2. **Template under the hood, custom price on top.** You pay $10k–$20k for a site that is mostly a theme.
3. **SEO talk, SEO rot.** They know SEO best practices but implement them on a slow stack that undercuts the rankings they are trying to win.
4. **Mixed incentives.** If they also run your ads, a faster-converting site cuts their billable spend. Not always aligned with your interest.

## FAQ

**Should I use separate shops for marketing and web?**
Usually yes. A specialized developer builds a better site. A specialized marketer runs better campaigns. They can work together without being the same company.

**Can you work alongside my marketing agency?**
Yes. I do this often. I handle the build and technical SEO, they handle campaigns and content.

**How do I tell if a marketing agency actually has developers?**
Ask who specifically will write the code. Ask to see their GitHub or recent build examples. Ask if the site will be custom code or a theme. Their answers will tell you.

**What if my marketing agency offers a "free" website?**
Nothing is free. Either it is priced into your retainer, or it is a template you outgrow in a year. Usually both.
