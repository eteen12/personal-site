## The Okanagan Lake pitch deck

A Kelowna business books a Vancouver agency. The kickoff deck has a photo of Okanagan Lake on the cover. Nice touch. Then the site copy talks about "lakeside vibes" in a way that tells you no one on the team has actually sat on a patio on Bernard Ave in July.

This is the quiet problem with remote Vancouver agencies serving Okanagan clients. The local texture is missing.

## How Vancouver agencies work

They are solid. Vancouver has real talent and serious agencies. They charge Vancouver rates because they sit in Vancouver offices on Vancouver leases and pay Vancouver salaries. They serve tech companies, national brands, and resource sector clients well.

For Okanagan small and mid-size businesses, that machine is often overbuilt and under-contextual.

## Where it breaks down

1. **No local context.** They do not know that a Mission winery has a different customer than one on the Westside. They do not know what "the bridge" means in traffic terms. That context shows up in copy, imagery, and local SEO.
2. **Vancouver pricing.** You pay Lower Mainland rates for work that does not need Lower Mainland overhead.
3. **They are not prioritizing you.** Bigger Vancouver clients get the A-team. Kelowna accounts often get the junior.
4. **Slower local loops.** Meeting in person for a shoot, a walk-through, a fitting session is a two-day trip, not an afternoon.
5. **Weaker local SEO signals.** Local SEO benefits from local links, local citations, local context. A Vancouver agency rarely has Kelowna roots to pull those from.

**Do Vancouver agencies ever make sense for Okanagan clients?**
Yes. If you are running a national campaign, working on large-scale branding, or need a very specialized creative team, the Vancouver depth is real.

**What about Kelowna agencies?**
There are good ones. I sometimes work alongside them as the developer. Worth checking if your project fits their wheelhouse.

Copy tone, neighbourhood references, local schema, citations, photos, service area pages, local event awareness. Small things that add up in Google's local pack and in how visitors feel about the brand.
