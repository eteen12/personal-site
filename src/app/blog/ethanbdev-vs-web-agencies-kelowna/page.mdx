## The Bernard Ave scenario

Picture this. A shop owner on Bernard Ave wants a new website. They walk into a downtown Kelowna agency. Three weeks later they have a proposal with an account manager, a project manager, a designer, a "strategist," and a junior dev somewhere in the stack. The bill is $28,000 and the timeline is four months.

That works for some businesses. It does not work for most of the ones I meet around the Mission or near Okanagan Lake.

## How Kelowna agencies actually work

Full-service agencies are built for scale. They win bigger clients, so they staff up with account managers, project managers, and specialists. That team needs to be fed. Which means the hourly rates go up. Which means your small brochure site now has five people on a Monday status call talking about it.

This is not a slam. Agencies do real work. They run bigger brand programs, ad campaigns, and enterprise contracts where that overhead is justified. The problem is when a local restaurant or contractor gets routed through the same machine.

## Where the agency model breaks down for Okanagan SMBs

1. **Slow communication.** You email the account manager. They email the PM. The PM emails the dev. By the time a one-line copy change ships, a week has passed.
2. **Overhead you pay for but do not see.** Office space downtown, a sales team, quarterly planning offsites. All of it goes into your hourly rate.
3. **Template under the hood.** Many agencies still ship WordPress themes with a stack of plugins. You pay custom prices and get a template site that gets slower every year.
4. **No real ownership.** Contract ends, and suddenly you need to pay them to make any change. You do not actually control your site.
