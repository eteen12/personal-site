It's small enough that most local businesses still get their sites built by a friend's nephew or a national template reseller, but the search market is maturing fast — especially around 30th Ave, the waterfront, and the retail strip heading toward Coldstream. A well-built Vernon site can dominate Google in a few months because the competition just isn't shipping solid work yet.

## 2. "North Okanagan Web Co." (local agency archetype)

The mid-size local agency with a storefront off 30th Ave and a team of four or five. They do brand, design, and basic Shopify or WordPress builds.

**Pros:** Design polish, process, comfort handling bigger brand projects.

**Cons:** Agency markup, slow turnaround, and a tendency to ship the same theme in different colours for every client. If you need custom software or real engineering, they'll subcontract it.

## 3. "Silver Star Freelance" (solo freelancer archetype)

One person in Vernon or Armstrong, charges reasonable rates, does decent work on small sites.

**Pros:** Cheap, direct, friendly.

**Cons:** Skill varies wildly. Some disappear during ski season at Silver Star. Ask for recent work with live URLs and check mobile speed — that'll tell you everything.

## 4. "Template Valley" (WordPress/Squarespace mill)

Flat-fee template shop, probably not based in Vernon but happy to take your money. You send logo and copy, they return a Divi site in two weeks.

**Pros:** Cheap, fast, works if you just want a digital business card.

**Cons:** Bloated, slow, mediocre SEO out of the box. The moment you want anything custom, you're stuck.

## 5. "Outsource International" (overseas archetype)

Pitches you on LinkedIn, quotes half what a local charges, does the work across a 13-hour time gap.

**Pros:** Cheapest option.

**Cons:** No local context — they won't know why ranking for "Vernon BC" matters more than ranking for just "Vernon", they'll miss nuances around Coldstream and Lumby, and edits take days of back-and-forth.

## Picking the right fit

Most Vernon businesses I talk to fall into one of three buckets:

1. **"I just need a site."** A template shop works. Don't overpay.
2. **"I want to rank and grow."** Hire a developer who understands local SEO, schema, and how Vernon search terms actually perform. That's me, or a good freelancer with a clean portfolio.
3. **"I need more than a site."** Booking systems, client portals, automations, AI voice or chat — that's engineering work. Get someone who's actually shipped software, not just themes.

If you're in bucket 2 or 3, it's worth a 20-minute conversation with me before you sign anything with anyone else.
